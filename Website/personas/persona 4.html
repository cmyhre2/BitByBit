Persona 4 — The Event Planner

A persona representing professionals who depend on precise, forward-looking forecasts to manage large-scale events where timing and accuracy are critical.

Overview

David, age 42, is a professional event planner who coordinates corporate retreats, weddings, and festivals. He manages high-stakes logistics where weather errors can have major financial consequences. David relies on multiple forecast sources, including direct communication with meteorologists, to ensure reliability.

Persona Details

Name: David

Age: 42

Occupation: Professional Event Planner

Location: Metropolitan area

Profile Summary: David handles large-scale events where weather decisions affect safety, cost, and client satisfaction. He uses multiple weather platforms and seeks tools that provide actionable data such as probability forecasts, micro-climate insights, and scenario-based planning options.

Goals:

  • Access long-range forecasts with confidence scores and probability ranges.
  • Receive real-time alerts when forecasts change significantly.
  • Gain micro-climate and venue-specific weather data for planning precision.

Frustrations:

  • Consumer apps that oversimplify or lack transparency about accuracy.
  • Inconsistent forecasts across different sources.
  • Limited long-range and micro-climate coverage for key event sites.

Needs:

  • Professional-grade forecasting tools that are accessible and easy to use.
  • Scenario modeling features to test alternative event timings.
  • Historical weather comparisons and risk analysis for informed planning.
Persona Snapshot
Name
David — The Event Planner
A meticulous planner who values accuracy, transparency, and actionable insights to manage weather-dependent events successfully.

Motivation: Prevent costly weather-related event failures through reliable forecasting.

Tech Use: Combines consumer apps, professional services, and direct meteorologist input.

Key takeaway
David needs professional-grade accuracy and advanced scenario tools—his livelihood depends on minimizing weather-related risk.

Design implications

  • Include probability-based long-range forecasting tools.
  • Enable significant forecast-change alerts and confidence scoring.
  • Offer venue-level micro-climate and historical weather analytics.
