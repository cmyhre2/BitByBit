Persona 3 — The Family Organizer

A persona representing multitasking parents who manage family schedules around constantly changing weather conditions.

Overview

Jennifer, age 38, is a parent of three who manages household logistics while working part-time. She checks the weather multiple times a day, often juggling data interpretation with family coordination. Jennifer wants simple, advisory-style insights that fit seamlessly into her busy routine.

Persona Details

Name: Jennifer

Age: 38

Occupation: Parent & Part-time Professional

Location: Suburban area

Profile Summary: Jennifer constantly plans around her children's schedules and outdoor activities. She checks weather 4–5 times daily but struggles with generic forecasts that require extra thought. She prefers quick, visual, and advisory weather cues to help her make family-related decisions faster.

Goals:

  • Get weather summaries in plain, actionable language.
  • Quickly know what her kids need for the day (jackets, rain gear, etc.).
  • Access at-a-glance widgets showing upcoming weather impacts.

Frustrations:

  • Generic forecasts that don’t translate to family planning needs.
  • Too many notifications about irrelevant weather details.
  • Having to interpret percentages and temperature swings manually.

Needs:

  • Advisory-style recommendations like “Pack layers” or “Rain gear needed at 3pm.”
  • Simple, icon-based widgets for fast, visual updates.
  • Smart notifications triggered only by meaningful weather changes.
Persona Snapshot
Name
Jennifer — The Family Organizer
A busy multitasker who manages kids, schedules, and household planning—she values clarity, convenience, and useful alerts over raw data.

Motivation: Simplify family planning and stay ahead of weather disruptions.

Tech Use: Uses Apple weather app, home screen widgets, and SMS alerts from schools.

Key takeaway
Jennifer values straightforward, visual, and context-aware weather guidance that fits her hectic, multitasking lifestyle.

Design implications

  • Develop home screen widgets with clear visual indicators for family activities.
  • Provide actionable, advisory-style messages instead of raw data.
  • Filter notifications to show only significant, plan-impacting weather events.
