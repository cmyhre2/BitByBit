Interview #4 — Event Planner

An in-depth interview exploring how professional event planners depend on weather insights, their pain points, and their needs for long-range, high-confidence forecasting tools.

Context

This interview reveals how critical weather precision is in event logistics, where timing, risk management, and financial stakes are all influenced by accurate, actionable forecasts. This interview was taken 10/15/25 by Chris Myhre.

Transcript

Q: Tell me about how weather impacts your work.

A: Weather can make or break an event. I plan everything from corporate retreats to outdoor weddings, and I'm constantly watching forecasts weeks in advance. A wrong call can cost tens of thousands of dollars.

Q: What weather tools do you rely on currently?

A: I use a mix. Weather.com for general forecasting, specialized services for longer-range predictions, and I literally call local meteorologists for important events. I need something more reliable than consumer apps but more accessible than enterprise solutions.

Q: What would make a weather app valuable for your professional needs?

A: Long-range accuracy - even if it's just probability ranges. Like "65% chance of rain-free day" two weeks out. I can work with probabilities, I just need honest data. Also, micro-climate information - venues can have very different conditions than the general area forecast.

Q: What specific data points matter most to you?

A: Rain probability, obviously. But also wind speeds for tents, temperature ranges for catering decisions, humidity for outdoor setups, and UV index for daytime events. I need hourly breakdowns, not daily summaries.

Q: What features would set an app apart for professional users like you?

A: Historical comparison - "this day last year was X" or "typical weather for this date." Confidence scores for forecasts. Alerts when the forecast changes significantly - like if rain probability jumps from 20% to 60%. And maybe even scenario planning - "if we move the event 3 hours earlier, conditions improve by X%."

Q: Would you pay for premium features?

A: Absolutely, if it saves me even one disaster. Accuracy and professional features are worth real money in my industry.

Design notes

  • Provide long-range forecasts with probabilistic data for planning confidence.
  • Include micro-climate and venue-specific forecasting options.
  • Display hourly forecasts and environmental metrics (wind, humidity, UV, temperature).
  • Offer historical weather patterns and typical-condition comparisons.
  • Enable real-time forecast change alerts with confidence levels.
  • Introduce scenario planning tools for schedule adjustment insights.
  • Monetize through professional premium features aimed at event planners.
