Interview #3 — Parent/Family Organizer

A detailed interview capturing how parents manage daily family plans around weather and what they'd want from a smarter, family-friendly forecast app.

Context

This interview explores the challenges of busy parents juggling multiple schedules and how weather apps can better support family decision-making through actionable insights and smart reminders. This interview was taken 10/10/25 by Jaleah Beason.

Transcript

Q: How does weather impact your daily planning with kids?

A: Oh my gosh, constantly. I'm planning outfits for three kids, deciding if soccer practice will happen, figuring out if we can go to the park after school. Weather basically dictates our whole day.

Q: What's your current weather-checking routine?

A: I check it first thing in the morning, then probably 3-4 more times during the day as plans develop. I use the Apple weather app mostly, but I also get texts from the school district about weather delays or cancellations.

Q: What frustrates you about current weather apps?

A: They're not designed for planning a family's day. I don't just need to know the forecast - I need to know "will my kids need jackets at 3pm pickup?" or "will little league be canceled?" I'm constantly doing mental math, like okay it says 40% rain at 5pm, do I pack rain gear for practice or not?

Q: If you could wave a magic wand, what would your ideal weather app do?

A: It would be more... advisory? Like "pack layers - big temperature swing today" or "outdoor activities looking good until 4pm." Also, I'd love activity-specific forecasts - is it good park weather? Pool weather? Maybe even integrate with my calendar and warn me if weather conflicts with our plans.

Q: What about widgets or quick-access features?

A: Yes! I want to glance at my home screen and immediately see what I need to know. Not just temperature - like a little visual that says "rain gear needed at 3pm" with an umbrella icon. Make it brain-dead simple because I'm usually doing five things at once.

Q: How do you feel about weather notifications?

A: Love them when they're useful, hate them when they're not. Don't tell me "it's sunny!" - I have windows. But DO tell me if weather is about to mess up my day. "Rain starting in 30 minutes" when I'm about to take kids to the park? Perfect.

Design notes

  • Focus on family-oriented, advisory-style forecasts rather than raw data.
  • Integrate activity-specific and time-aware guidance (e.g., school pickup, sports, playtime).
  • Enable calendar syncing for proactive weather conflict alerts.
  • Design visual, low-effort widgets for quick daily context.
  • Send actionable notifications that highlight meaningful changes only.
  • Include outfit and gear suggestions based on temperature swings and precipitation.
Interviewee snapshot
Profile
Parent/Family Organizer
Busy parent managing multiple kids’ schedules, relying on quick, clear weather insights to plan daily activities and logistics.
Notifications: Only when plans are affected
Needs: Family planning, activity-based context
Key takeaway
Parents want weather apps that simplify complex planning into actionable advice—what to wear, when to go, and how weather will impact the day’s schedule.

Next steps

  • Prototype “advisory” forecast cards (e.g., outfit, activity, timing).
  • Develop family calendar integration for automated conflict detection.
  • Test widget and quick-access dashboard designs with multitasking users.
