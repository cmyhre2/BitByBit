Interview #2 — Outdoor Enthusiast

A deep-dive interview exploring how serious outdoor adventurers plan trips around weather, their tools, and what they wish weather apps did better.

Context

This interview highlights the needs of a data-driven user who values precision, elevation-based forecasts, severe alerts, and real-world reliability in outdoor settings. This interview was taken 10/7/25 by Joshua Henderson.

Transcript

Q: I see you're an avid hiker. How does weather factor into your planning?

A: It's everything. I check weather obsessively before any trip - sometimes starting a week out. I need to know temperatures at different elevations, wind speeds, chance of storms, visibility. Bad weather data can literally be dangerous in the mountains.

Q: What apps or tools do you currently use?

A: I use like three different apps and cross-reference them. Weather Underground for hyper-local data, Mountain-Forecast for elevation-specific info, and then I check NOAA directly for serious trips. No single app does it all.

Q: What would make you consolidate to just one app?

A: Accuracy first - I need to trust it. Then I'd want elevation-based forecasts, real wind data not just "breezy," and trail-specific conditions if possible. Also, hour-by-hour breakdowns, not just daily summaries. If it says 30% chance of rain, I need to know if that's morning or afternoon.

Q: What about features beyond just forecast data?

A: Hmm. Historical data would be cool - like "this trail is usually X degrees colder than the base" based on patterns. Or crowd-sourced conditions - if someone just hiked that trail, what did they experience? Oh, and sunrise/sunset times with golden hour marked for photography.

Q: How important are severe weather alerts for your activities?

A: Critical. But they need to be location-specific. I don't care if there's a tornado warning two counties over. I need lightning alerts within like a 5-mile radius when I'm on an exposed ridge. And give me enough lead time to get to safety.

Design notes

  • Emphasize reliability and scientific accuracy of forecasts.
  • Include elevation-aware and trail-specific weather modeling.
  • Offer hour-by-hour breakdowns and historical weather patterns.
  • Integrate community reports for real trail conditions and safety updates.
  • Provide hyper-local lightning and severe weather alerts with early warnings.
  • Display sunrise, sunset, and golden-hour timing for outdoor photographers.
