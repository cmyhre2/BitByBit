Interview #1 — Morning Commuter

A short user interview capturing how a typical commuter checks weather and what they'd want from a better weather app.

Context

This interview focuses on everyday needs: quick glanceability, commute-aware alerts, multi-location awareness, and actionable advice (e.g., what to wear). This interview was taken 10/6/25 by Chris Myhre.

Transcript

Q: Thanks for taking the time to chat. Can you tell me a bit about how you currently check the weather?

A: Sure! I usually just glance at my iPhone's default weather app while I'm getting ready. Sometimes I'll check again before I leave to see if I need an umbrella or a jacket.

Q: What works well about that routine?

A: It's quick, I guess. It's right there on my phone. But honestly, it's wrong a lot. It'll say sunny and then I'm caught in rain by 3pm.

Q: That sounds frustrating. If you could design your perfect weather app, what would it do differently?

A: I'd love something that tells me "hey, it's going to rain during your commute home, bring an umbrella." Like, don't just tell me it's going to rain sometime today - tell me when it matters for me. Also, I drive 30 minutes to work, and the weather can be totally different there than at my house. I'd want it to know both locations.

Q: What about notifications - do you want the app reaching out to you, or would you rather check it yourself?

A: Notifications are fine, but only if they're actually useful. I don't need to know it's 72 degrees. I need to know if something is changing that affects my plans. Like "rain starting in 20 minutes" when I'm about to leave work.

Q: Anything else you wish weather apps did better?

A: Yeah - just be more accurate! And maybe tell me what to wear? Like "light jacket recommended" instead of making me figure out what 58 degrees feels like.

Design notes

  • Priority: commute-aware, time-specific alerts rather than generic daily summaries.
  • Support multiple saved locations (home, work) and compare forecasts side-by-side.
  • Push notifications only for meaningful changes (rain during commute, rapid temp shifts, severe alerts).
  • Human-friendly advisories ("light jacket recommended", "umbrella for evening commute").
Interviewee snapshot
Profile
Morning Commuter
Typical routine: checks phone app while getting ready; drives ~30 minutes to work
Notifications: Prefers only meaningful alerts
Preferences: Commute-aware, multi-location, actionable advice
Key takeaway
Users want weather information that ties to their schedule and locations, and that tells them what to do (umbrella, jacket), not raw numbers.

Next steps

  • Prototype commute-aware alert UI and test with local users.
  • Implement multi-location comparison in the app's home view.
  • Add simple clothing/advice layer driven by temp+precipitation rules.
